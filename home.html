Green cloud computing refers to the potential environmental benefits cloud-based services are able to offer society. There are 3 main goals of green cloud computing:

  • Maximizing energy efficiencies during the device's lifecycle
  • Promoting the use of recyclable materials
  • Minimizing the use of hazardous IT components

This can be observed/realized from 2 perspectives:

  • Green hardware - Energy-efficient and environmentally friendly information and communications technology tools such as servers, network appliances, and storage devices used in datacenters. It also comprises the power supply units, the cooling equipment, and the physical building that houses these components.
  • Green software engineering-methodologies - Includes all applications that manage datacenters and other cloud-based services. Main idea behind green software engineering methodologies is to build reliable applications that not only meet organizations requirements but are also energy efficient.
